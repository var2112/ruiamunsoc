Committees

Lok Sabha

Deliberation on electoral reforms in India

a) Promotion of free and fair elections

b) Addressing the threats to free democracy due to rampant defections

Introduction to the Committee Lok Sabha is a very zestful committee. Also known as the House of People, it comprises the representatives of the people from all over the country, thus its sundry nature. It is the lower house of the Indian bicameral legislature where the members are elected directly by Universal Adult Franchise. All members represent their respective constituencies and hold the office for a period of five years. Although not a part of the United Nations, Lok Sabha is at the core of the union legislature and hence a significant component of Indian Democracy. It is a platform where the Members of Parliament discuss and deliberate about various important issues in order to come up with effective solutions.
About the Agenda Deliberations on electoral reforms in India
• Promotion of free and fair elections.
• Addressing the threats to free democracy due to rampant defections. Lok Sabha itself is a product of the elaborate electoral process in India and hence it needs to focus on the gravity of the free and fair elections. Overcoming all the obstacles in way such as defections and other electoral malpractices is the only way forward.

United Nations Security Council

Addressing unlawful military aggression by powerful nations with special emphasis on Chinese skirmishes.

United Nations' establishment was a radical act, as for the first time, nations gave up an element of their sovereignty to be bound by decisions of the Security Council, and also conferred on the Council global authority for the  use of force. United Nations primary responsibility is maintaining international peace and security and to take necessary actions, whenever peace is threatened for which the UNSC works proactively for.
Introduction to the Agenda :- "Addressing Unlawful Military aggression by the powerful nations with special emphasis on Chinese Skirmishes." In international relations, aggression is an act or policy of expansion carried out by one state at the expense of another by means of an unprovoked and unlawful military attack for varied interests.Though they've come at huge costs. And therefore for which the United Nations is always striving to bring the influential powers at the table to stop any Skirmishes or conflicts that may provoke into any form of aggression for existing or for past reasons.

United Nations Human Rights Council

Finding the solutions to the refugee crisis with special emphasis on Syrian & African situation.

Introduction to the Committee The UNHRC- United Nations Human Rights Council is an “inter-governmental body within the United Nations system responsible for strengthening the promotion and protection of human rights around the globe and for addressing situations of human rights violations and make recommendations on them.” It has the ability to discuss all thematic human rights issues and situations that require its attention throughout the year.
Introduction to the Agenda Every day, all over the world, people make one of the most difficult decisions in their lives: to leave their homes in search of a safer, better life. The agenda tabled for this session of the UNHRC at the Ruia MUN 2022 is - Finding the solutions for refugee crisis with special emphasis on the Syrian & African situation.

United Nations Women

Abortion as a human right: Access to safe and legal abortions.

Introduction to the Committee: UN Women is the United Nations organization that works on issues related to women empowerment and gender equality. It is a global organization that works for the socio-economic upliftment of women and girls and was established to accelerate progress in meeting their needs worldwide.
Introduction to the Agenda: Abortion as a human right: access to safe and legal abortions. • Abortion Laws across the globe • Deliberations on how access to the Abortion process can be increased and made safe.

International Press

Committee where delegates would be able to observe the debates
and proceedings of all the committees and role play as journalists

The International Press was established in the year 1948 in the UN, and it has always strived to bring out the truth to the people. The Press acts as a link between the masses and the UN. The Press has its own stand, and it has become a necessity for it to provide factual and enlightening information, which frees people from ignorance. The most important function is that it acts a watchdog, and keeps the decision makers in line, and holds the answerable and responsible for any kind of actions taken.
As members of the IPD, you will be a part of a simulation unlike any other at RUIAMUN. Instead of representing the interests of a single country, the International Press Delegation allows participants to act as unbiased journalists, reporting on the proceedings of each simulation in the Conference. You will investigate committee debates, conduct interviews, edit articles, and cover press conferences.
